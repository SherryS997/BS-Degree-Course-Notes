### Project Aims

- Aimed to develop a platform to automatically segment background/foreground and extract non-overlapping objects from real-world images.

### Project Description

- **The Summer Vision Project** is an attempt to use our summer workers effectively in the construction of a significant part of a visual system.

## Key Milestones
- **ImageNet arrives:** The ImageNet dataset is introduced, becoming a standard benchmark for image recognition tasks.
- **AlexNet wins the ImageNet challenge:** AlexNet, developed by Alex Krizhevsky et al., significantly outperformed other methods and popularized deep learning in computer vision.
- **A CNN, variant of ZFNet, wins ImageNet challenge:** ZFNet, developed by Zeiler and Fergus, further advanced the state-of-the-art in image classification.
- **R-CNNs for object detection arrive:** Region-based Convolutional Neural Networks (R-CNNs) were introduced by Ross Girshick et al. for object detection tasks.
- **Understanding CNNs begins:** Efforts to interpret and understand the behavior of CNNs start gaining traction.
- **InceptionNet, VGG models arrive:** GoogLeNet (InceptionNet) by Szegedy et al. and VGG models by Simonyan and Zisserman mark significant advancements in network architectures.
- **Human Pose Estimation CNNs:** Models focusing on human pose estimation are developed.
- **Deep generative models: GANs, VAEs:** Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) by Goodfellow et al. and Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) by Kingma and Welling are introduced.
- **ResNet arrives:** Residual Networks (ResNet) by He et al. solve the vanishing gradient problem and achieve superior performance.
- **CNNs match human performance on ImageNet:** Convolutional Neural Networks achieve human-level performance on the ImageNet challenge.
- **FCN, SegNet and U-Net for semantic segmentation:** Fully Convolutional Networks (FCNs), SegNet, and U-Net for semantic segmentation are introduced, marking significant progress in image segmentation.
- **COCO dataset arrives:** The Common Objects in Context (COCO) dataset is introduced, providing a rich dataset for object detection, segmentation, and captioning.
- **VQA dataset arrives:** The Visual Question Answering (VQA) dataset is introduced, focusing on understanding and reasoning about images.
